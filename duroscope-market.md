<p><p>The Duroscope market encompasses various hardness testing methods used primarily in material characterization. The Brinell Duroscope employs a hard steel or carbide ball indenter to measure hardness based on the size of the indentation left in the material. The Vickers Duroscope uses a diamond pyramid indenter for similar measurements, allowing for testing on a wider range of materials. The Rockwell Duroscope simplifies the process with a spring-loaded indenter, providing quick hardness readings. Other testing methods may include specialized or advanced techniques suited for niche applications.</p></p>
