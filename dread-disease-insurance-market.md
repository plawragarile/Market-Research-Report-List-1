<p><p>Dread Disease Insurance primarily encompasses two market types: fixed-term insurance and whole-life insurance. Fixed-term insurance provides coverage for a specified period, ensuring policyholders receive a lump sum payout if diagnosed with a covered critical illness during that term. In contrast, whole-life insurance offers lifelong protection, accumulating cash value over time and promising a payout upon diagnosis or the policyholder's death, whichever comes first. Both types help mitigate financial risks associated with severe health conditions.</p></p>
