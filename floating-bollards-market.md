<p><p>Floating bollards, designed to protect waterfronts and docks, come primarily in two types: cast iron and cast steel. Cast iron bollards are favored for their durability and aesthetic appeal, making them suitable for urban landscapes. In contrast, cast steel bollards offer higher tensile strength and are ideal for heavy-duty applications, such as commercial ports. Both materials provide corrosion resistance, but cast steel generally has enhanced resistance to impact and harsh environmental conditions, catering to diverse operational needs in marine settings.</p></p>
