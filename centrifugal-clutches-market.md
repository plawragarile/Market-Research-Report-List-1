<p><p>Centrifugal clutches are categorized into three main types: mechanical, spring, and others. Mechanical centrifugal clutches typically use a system of weights that engage the clutch as speed increases, providing a direct connection. Spring centrifugal clutches rely on spring mechanisms to disengage at lower speeds, allowing for smoother transitions. Other types may incorporate innovative designs or materials to enhance performance for specific applications, catering to diverse industrial needs. Each type plays a crucial role in automotive, machinery, and equipment functionality.</p></p>
